## 2. What ACX Is

ACX is infrastructure connecting African content creators with advertisers.
ACX operates as a broker and arbiter between the two parties — it curates
which Creators are eligible for which tiers and indices, matches Advertiser
campaigns to compatible Creators, processes payments via licensed payment
partners, and arbitrates disputes under the protocol in Section 9. ACX is not
a media company, and does not itself produce or own the content published by
Creators.

## 3. Eligibility & Registration

- You must be able to enter a binding contract in your jurisdiction to
  register.
- Creators must provide accurate identity, contact, country, platform, and
  payout information at registration. Every Creator is placed at the
  **Emerging** tier by default, regardless of self-reported statistics, and
  moves to a higher tier only once their Verified Monthly Audience (VMA) is
  confirmed — via platform API where connected, or a review process where
  self-reported.
- Advertisers must provide accurate company, billing, and contact information.
  Accounts are subject to review before large campaigns can be booked.
- ACX reserves the right to decline or suspend registration where information
  provided is inaccurate or where a party fails required verification.

## 4. Freedom of Information Principle

- A Creator is free to publish any content on their own channel, subject only
  to a baseline legal and moral standard (nothing illegal, nothing hateful,
  nothing violating this Section) — not subject to ACX gatekeeping over
  commercial content generally.
- ACX will never require a Creator to keep the terms of their own earnings,
  rates, or contracts with ACX confidential. Creators may disclose what they
  are paid and on what terms.
- Any restriction on which categories or brands a Creator will carry through
  ACX arises only from the Creator's own opt-in exclusion list (Section 6) or
  a voluntary, paid exclusivity arrangement (Section 8) — never imposed by
  default.

## 5. Tiers and Indices

- Creators are placed into tiers (Emerging, Established, Premium, Flagship)
  based on Verified Monthly Audience and, for Premium/Flagship, delivery
  track record. Tier determines the standard ad-length ceiling a Creator can
  carry and whether custom/negotiated terms are available (Flagship only).
- Tier movement is reviewed periodically (not instantly) against confirmed
  data, to prevent short-term spikes from causing premature tier changes.
- Creators may be included in topic-based indices (e.g. Business, Tech,
  Culture) or, where cross-topical, in ACX Faces — indexed by audience across
  all their content rather than by subject matter.
- Index inclusion and tier placement are determined by ACX according to its
  published methodology, and may be updated as that methodology evolves.

## 6. Exclusions

- At registration and at any time thereafter, a Creator may declare categories
  or brand types they will not carry ads for (e.g. alcohol, betting, tobacco,
  political campaigns, adult products).
- ACX will not knowingly match a campaign against a Creator's declared
  exclusions. ACX is not liable for an Advertiser's independent, non-ACX
  dealings with a Creator that fall outside this platform.

## 7. Campaigns, Deliverables & Approval

- An Advertiser supplies an ad asset (video or photo slides) of an agreed
  length and a voice-over brief. The Creator localizes the asset in their own
  voice and language as part of their content.
- A Creator's tier sets the maximum deliverable length they can carry, based
  on audience and content format; this is enforced at booking.
- The Creator submits a draft for review against the brief. The payout and
  duration clock described in Section 8 begins only once this draft is
  **approved** — not at first submission.
- As part of submission, the Creator completes a brief checklist reflecting
  brand fit and any concerns, which becomes part of ACX's records for
  brand-safety and rating purposes.

## 8. Payment Terms

- Advertisers fund the full estimated cost of a campaign upfront, held and
  processed through ACX's licensed payment partner(s) — ACX does not hold
  advertiser or creator funds directly in its own accounts.
- Payout to the Creator releases in two tranches: a majority share on
  approved publish, and the remainder at the end of an agreed minimum
  duration the content must remain live.
- If content is removed before the agreed duration ends, the remaining
  tranche is prorated to the portion of the duration actually honored, except
  where removal resulted from circumstances outside the Creator's reasonable
  control (e.g. a platform strike, copyright claim, or account suspension
  not caused by the Creator).
- ACX's commission is calculated as a percentage of total campaign spend,
  varying by Creator tier, and is deducted from each tranche as it releases —
  ACX does not collect its full commission upfront.
- Creators may voluntarily accept paid exclusivity arrangements at the
  Flagship/Faces tier (Section 9), which carry additional, separately agreed
  commercial terms.
- Payout method and currency follow the Creator's registered country payment
  configuration. Campaigns may only be booked in countries ACX has confirmed
  as payment-ready; a country's inclusion in ACX's content index does not by
  itself mean campaigns can be completed there.

## 9. Exclusivity (Flagship / Faces Tier)

- Standard-tier Creators carry no exclusivity obligation to ACX or any
  Advertiser and may take on other sponsorships freely, through ACX or
  independently.
- A Flagship or Faces-tier Creator may voluntarily accept a defined,
  time-limited exclusivity arrangement (e.g. not carrying a competing
  category for an agreed period) in exchange for additional compensation.
  Such arrangements are separately negotiated and documented.
- Exclusivity arrangements are subject to a morals clause permitting
  suspension or termination of that specific arrangement — not the Creator's
  general ACX account — if the Creator's conduct materially damages the
  associated brand's reputation.

## 10. Fulfillment Failures & Dispute Protocol

- **No delivery:** if a Creator does not submit content by the agreed
  deadline, no payout is made, the Advertiser is refunded, and the failure is
  recorded against the Creator's delivery history.
- **Content not matching the brief:** addressed at the pre-publish review
  stage (Section 7); no payout is triggered until an approved version exists.
- **Defective or conflicting Advertiser asset:** addressed at booking via
  format-compatibility and exclusion checks (Sections 6–7).
- **Platform-side failure** (takedown, strike, suspension not caused by the
  Creator): ACX will establish and confirm the cause, and determine a remedy
  — republishing, delayed republishing, or aborting the placement with
  payout prorated to the portion of duration actually honored. **ACX holds
  final arbitration authority in determining cause and remedy under this
  Section.**

## 12. Data Protection & Security

Data protection is treated as a core commitment, not boilerplate — the
following applies in addition to, not instead of, whatever specific law

  connections and audience statistics, category and content preferences,
  exclusion settings, payout method details, and campaign/transaction history.
- **Why it's collected:** solely to operate the platform as described in

  dispute resolution. Data is not collected for purposes beyond these without

- **Security commitments:** data in transit and at rest is encrypted;
  access to personal and payout data is restricted on a need-to-know basis

  partners under their own regulated security standards, not stored directly

  platform — licensed payment processors (for payout), and, where a Creator

- **User rights:** users may request access to, correction of, or deletion of
  their personal data, subject to what ACX is legally required to retain

- **Incident response:** in the event of a data breach affecting personal

### Note on This Draft

This document translates the structural decisions made throughout ACX's
design process (tiers, freedom-of-information principle, payment tranches,
exclusions, fulfillment protocol, payment-readiness gating) into
Terms-and-Conditions form.

Sections 11 (IP), 13 (Liability), and 14 (Termination) now carry complete,
industry-standard language rather than placeholders. Section 12 (Data
Protection) is written as a substantive commitment given its priority, though
it still needs country-specific legal alignment. Section 16 (Governing Law)
remains deliberately generic, with `[bracketed placeholders]` for the actual
jurisdiction, venue, and arbitral body once ACX's entity structure is
settled — this is the one section that cannot be made meaningful without
that decision first.

Note on enforceability: broad liability disclaimers like Section 13's are
standard practice and a reasonable starting position, but most jurisdictions
will not enforce a total exclusion of liability — courts typically preserve
exceptions for things like fraud, gross negligence, or statutory consumer
protections regardless of what the contract states. That's normal, not a
drafting flaw, and doesn't reduce the practical value of having the clause.

Qualified legal counsel should still review this in full before use,
particularly Section 12 against each specific country's data protection law
and Section 16 once ACX's incorporation jurisdiction is decided.
