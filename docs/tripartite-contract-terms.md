# ACX Tripartite Contract — Terms Reference
*Consolidates every contractual term decided this session. A structural reference for drafting the real agreement — not a substitute for review by a contracts lawyer in each operating jurisdiction.*

---

## Parties

1. **ACX** — the platform, acting as broker/arbiter between advertiser and creator.
2. **The Advertiser** — funds a campaign and supplies the ad asset/brief.
3. **The Creator** — publishes the localized ad within their own content.

ACX is not a party to a simple two-sided transaction — it holds distinct
obligations to each side, and its own obligations (curation, arbitration,
payout timing) are enforceable terms in this agreement, not just service
promises.

---

## 1. Freedom of Information Principle

- The Creator is free to publish any content on their own channel, subject
  only to a baseline moral/legal floor (nothing illegal, nothing hateful,
  nothing violating platform policy) — not subject to ACX or third-party
  gatekeeping over commercial content.
- ACX never restricts what a Creator says on their own channel, and never
  gags a Creator from disclosing their own earnings, rates, or contract terms.
- Any restriction on which brands/categories a Creator will carry exists
  **only where the Creator has voluntarily agreed to it** — an opt-in
  exclusion list, or a paid exclusivity term (see Section 5). Restriction by
  paid consent, never by default.

---

## 2. Deliverable & Asset Terms

- The Advertiser supplies a silent ad asset (video or photo slides) of an
  agreed length (15s / 30s / 60s, or negotiated for Flagship/Faces tier) plus
  a voice-over brief.
- The Creator localizes the asset into their own voice/language as they
  integrate it into their content.
- **Format-compatibility gate:** a Creator's tier sets a hard ceiling on
  deliverable length based on their content format (e.g. a short-form video
  cannot carry a 60-second insert). This is enforced at booking, not
  discovered after recording.
- **Preview/approval step:** the Creator submits a draft for review against
  the brief before publishing counts as "approved publish." The payout and
  duration clock (Section 4) does not start until approval, so a legitimate
  revision cycle does not cost the Creator time.
- **Creator feedback/rating checklist:** at submission, the Creator completes
  a short checklist reflecting brand fit and any concerns — protects the
  Creator (a timestamped record of concerns raised) and feeds ACX's internal
  brand-safety and Creator Rating data.

---

## 3. Exclusions

- At registration, the Creator declares categories/brand types they will not
  carry (e.g. alcohol, betting, tobacco, political, adult products) — opt-in,
  Creator-controlled, not imposed.
- ACX will not match a campaign against a Creator's declared exclusions.
- The Advertiser's asset must be reviewed against the Creator's exclusion list
  before booking is confirmed — a defective or non-compliant match should be
  caught at booking, not discovered after a Creator has already recorded a
  voice-over.

---

## 4. Payment & Duration Terms

- **Tranche schedule:** payout releases in two parts —
  - **Tranche 1** (majority share, e.g. 90%) on approved publish.
  - **Tranche 2** (remainder, e.g. 10%) at the end of an agreed minimum
    duration the content must remain live (e.g. 100 days from approved
    publish).
- **No-delete clause:** the Creator agrees the published ad content will
  remain live for the agreed duration. This does not, and cannot, guarantee
  the Creator will not delete it — it creates a remedy if they do.
- **Prorated penalty, not all-or-nothing:** if content is removed before the
  duration ends, Tranche 2 is prorated to the portion of the duration actually
  honored (e.g. live 60 of 100 days → 60% of Tranche 2), not forfeited
  entirely regardless of when removal happened.
- **Legitimate exceptions carved out:** removal due to a platform strike,
  copyright claim, account suspension, or other cause outside the Creator's
  control does not count against the Creator and does not trigger the
  proration penalty.
- **ACX's commission follows the same tranche schedule as the Creator's
  payout** — not collected upfront in full. ACX earns its share as the
  campaign actually delivers, not before.
- **Commission rate is set by Creator tier** (Emerging/Established/Premium/
  Flagship/Faces — see the ACX Rate Calculator framework), disclosed to the
  Creator as part of their earnings ledger, though not necessarily disclosed
  to the Advertiser as a line item (the Advertiser sees one all-in price).
- **Payout method and rail** follow the Creator's registered country payment
  configuration (see the Payment Protocol document) — mobile money, bank
  transfer, or other rail as applicable and confirmed payment-ready for that
  country.

---

## 5. Exclusivity (Flagship / Faces tier only)

- Standard tiers carry no exclusivity obligation — a Creator may take on any
  other sponsorship, through ACX or independently, at any time.
- A Creator may voluntarily accept a paid exclusivity term (e.g. "no
  competing beverage sponsorship for 90 days") in exchange for a retainer or
  premium rate, structured similarly to a traditional endorsement deal.
- Exclusivity terms must be specific (category, duration, scope) and are a
  negotiated commercial term — never a default condition of joining ACX.
- **Morals clause:** applies specifically to Flagship/Faces exclusivity
  arrangements, given the higher brand-safety exposure of a cross-topical
  personality under sustained brand association. Allows suspension or
  termination of the exclusivity arrangement (not the Creator's ACX account
  generally) if the Creator's conduct materially damages the associated
  brand's reputation.

---

## 6. Fulfillment Failure Protocol

Applies when something goes wrong mid-campaign:

1. **No delivery at all** — deadline passes with no submission. No payout
   processed; full refund to Advertiser; recorded against Creator's delivery
   track record (feeds tier-eligibility history).
2. **Delivered but doesn't match brief** — caught at the preview/approval step
   (Section 2) before the clock starts; no payout until a matching version is
   approved.
3. **Advertiser asset defective or excluded-category conflict** — caught at
   booking via the format-compatibility and exclusion checks (Sections 2–3),
   not after recording has begun.
4. **Platform-side failure** (takedown, strike, suspension) — protocol:
   - Establish cause.
   - Confirm cause.
   - Remedy: republish immediately → republish after an agreed delay →
     publishing aborted → aborted publishing confirmed → no payout processed
     (for the affected tranche only, prorated per Section 4 where partial
     duration was honored).
   - **ACX holds arbitration authority** to establish and confirm cause under
     this protocol — stated explicitly here so it is not left implicit or
     later disputed by either party.

---

## 7. Country & Payment Readiness

- A campaign may only be booked in a country flagged **payment-ready** in
  ACX's country payment configuration (see Payment Protocol document,
  Section 6). Content may be indexed in Discover for a country before it is
  payment-ready, but Trade campaigns cannot complete there until confirmed.

---

## 8. Open Items Still Requiring Legal Drafting

- Actual binding contract language (this document is a structural term
  reference, not the agreement itself).
- Jurisdiction and dispute-resolution venue per operating country.
- Data protection terms for Creator/Advertiser information collected at
  registration, aligned with each country's data protection law (e.g. Kenya's
  DPA 2019, and equivalents elsewhere).
- Termination terms — how either party exits the relationship, and what
  happens to in-flight campaigns at that point.
